2020 marked the year, when all humanity was connected and forced to change. Everyone has a story to tell as a result of it and here is mine… For the first time in my life, i experienced what its like to have your plans not work out, as if that has never happened before. Maybe it was different this time or maybe it was the same as an infant learning to walk, but constantly being greeted by the gravitational pull of failure. Isolated from the metaphysical world, i was left alone with my thoughts and my laptop. I finally had the time to explore my curiosities even further and at the end of it, i find someone, i found myself. As cheesy as that sounds, its remarkable what you can accomplish through self-reflection and critical thinking. I found myself questioning things and through that, a moment euphoria dawned upon me. Years ago i had ventured into self learning because i had questions in my mind to begin with. Questioned fueled through curiosity and frustration. Questions to which i later found the answers through various resources from podcasts, articles, videos and books. The euphoric moment was connected to a course that i purchased on Udemy. In that course i learned about Rational Emotive Behavior Therapy (REBT) and Neuro Linguistic Programming (NLP). At the core of REBT lies questions, and NLP supplements it by providing the answers. The initial questions that led me on the path of self learning are:

  1. How Can I Learn Faster?
  2. Why Do People Struggle With Money?
  3. Is College The Only Way?
  4. How Can I Improve My Brain Health?
  5. How Can I Apply What I Learn?
  6. Who Would I Be Without My Excuses?

The only difference this time around was that the question on my mind was, “what do i want to do with my life?” I might have a college degree, but i know that its useless. Why would i believe that a person would magically become valuable after purchasing something, and a piece of paper at that? To my suprise, magical thinking is a thing and its a cognitive distortion. Throughout my exploring i was able to find out that what i truly wanted to do was in the realm of technology as a knowledge worker. Making music popped into my mind because i reminisce the good times of being in the highschool symphony chorale. Writing for a living is something that i dabbled in as well…

Lessons

  1. When a person is unable to change a situation, they are force to change themselves.
  2. Health is the greatest wealth.
  3. Financial freedom is better than financial security.
  4. The world doesnt need us, but we need the world and everything in it.
  5. We cant appreaciate new things in our lives, if we dont appreaciate what we already have (Gratitude).

Its a short list of lessons that stick out to me, but i am aware that there is more - like this one.
